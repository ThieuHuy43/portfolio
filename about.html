About Me

Hi! I'm Huy, a final-year student passionate about Artificial Intelligence, particularly in Large Language Models (LLMs) and Computer Vision. I love building end-to-end AI systems that solve real-world problems.

Background

Currently pursuing my degree in AI/ML, I've focused my studies on deep learning, natural language processing, and computer vision. My academic journey has been enriched by hands-on research and practical projects that bridge theory and application.

My graduation project, DentalGPT, combines my interests in healthcare and AI. I fine-tuned an 8B parameter LLM using LoRA and built a RAG pipeline to create an intelligent assistant for dental healthcare in Vietnamese. This project taught me the intricacies of model optimization, retrieval systems, and production deployment.

Research Interests

Large Language Models & GenAI

I'm fascinated by how LLMs can be adapted to specific domains through fine-tuning techniques like LoRA and QLoRA. My focus is on making these powerful models more accessible and applicable to real-world use cases, particularly in low-resource languages like Vietnamese.

Few-Shot & Continual Learning

My research on AMAF-Net explores how models can learn new concepts from limited data while retaining previous knowledge. This work on Few-Shot Class-Incremental Learning (FSCIL) with multimodal alignment earned recognition at ICAI 2025.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

I believe RAG systems are key to making LLMs more reliable and grounded. I enjoy designing efficient retrieval pipelines using vector databases like ChromaDB and FAISS, and optimizing chunking strategies for better context retrieval.

What Drives Me

I'm motivated by the potential of AI to make a meaningful impact. Whether it's building a healthcare assistant, creating recommendation systems, or advancing research in continual learning, I aim to develop solutions that are both technically sound and practically useful.

I value end-to-end thinking—from data collection and model training to evaluation and deployment. I believe great AI systems require not just good models, but thoughtful engineering, rigorous evaluation, and user-centric design.
